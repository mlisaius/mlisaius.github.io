Intro

I am broadly interested in food, climate, gender, decolonization and machine learning.

I spend most of my time focusing on a spatial perspective, often from a satellite remote sensing perspective. My current work is focused on applications of self-supervised learning (which I see as an impersonalizing misnomer) for understanding large landscape systems. I am worried about the ways that SSL may be used to target minority and vulnerable groups.

I envision a food soverign future where people have access to (in the Ribot & Peluso sense) healthy food from their area, produced justly, and a healthy environment. En route to this vision, I am working on technology aids to help make sure the most vulnerable people on the planet (the 78% who rely on subsistance agriculture) can survive while bigger systems change happens.

I come from a background of community forest management, interdisciplinary approaches to environmental science and intersectional feminist geography perspectives. I would like to continue to interact with these spaces.

When away from screens, I spend most of my time dancing, being outside, cooking new recipes (please send your plant-based low FODMAP magic) and trying to live more conciously and slowly each day. I am working on mastering the Spanish language and making a decent fermented hot sauce.

About

Before joining the AI4ER CDT at Cambridge, I was the Lead Data Scientist for topics of remote sensing, food and climate and Tom Ford Fellow at The Rockefeller Foundation. I graduated from Stanford University in 2019 with a B.S. with Honors in Earth Systems and an M.S. in Earth Systems with a focus on remote sensing for applications in macro land systems and especially agriculture. During my time at Stanford, I spent four years collaborating with indigenous women in the Ecuadorian Amazon as a National Geographic Young Explorer understanding working on projects of forest disturbance, women's empowerment through new land management regimes and the accute limitations and opportunities of Earth Observation for community-lead management.

I grew up in the beautiful Pacific Northwest around Seattle Washington; temperate rainforests will always feel like home. I spent beautiful years across Latin America, and part of my heart stays in the high Andes. I lived in NYC for the first years of the pandemic, and have deep afinity with Fort Greene and Downtown Broolyn.
